MEDIA RELEASE 
 
RELIANCE POWER BOARD APPROVES THE FORMATION OF 
‘BOARD OF MANAGEMENT’ (BOM) TO FURTHER STRENGTHEN GOVERNANCE AND 
STRATEGIC BUSINESS OVERSIGHT 
 
THE BOARD OF MANAGEMENT COMPRISES OF CEO, KEY MANAGERIAL PERSONS 
AND OTHER BUSINESS LEADERS 
 
RELIANCE POWER BOARD NOTED THE INVESTMENTS IN FUTURE GROWTH AREAS OF 
RELIANCE NU ENERGIES.  
 
THE COMPANY HAS EMERGED AS THE LARGEST PLAYER IN SOLAR + BESS (ENERGY 
STORAGE) ON A COMPETITIVE BID BASIS WITH A TOTAL CAPACITY OF UPTO 4 GW 
SOLAR AND UPTO 6.5 GW OF BESS 
 
Mumbai, November 19, 2025:  Reliance Power, one of India’s leading private sector power 
companies, today announced that its Board of Directors, at their meeting held on November 19, 
2025, has approved the constitution of the Board of Management (BOM) comprising of the CEO, 
Key Managerial Personnel, and senior business leaders of the Company. 
 
The creation of the Board of Management represents a step in Reliance Power’s pursuit of 
stronger governance, sharper oversight mechanisms and build a more agile and future- ready 
organisation. The initiative reflects the Company’s commitment to adopting best -in-class 
governance practices that support long-term value creation for all stakeholders. 
 
Reliance Power also highlighted the emerging growth opportunities within its subsidiary, Reliance 
NU Energies. The Company has emerged as the largest player in Solar + BESS (Battery Energy 
Storage Systems) on a com petitive bid basis, with an awarded capacity of up to 4  GW of solar 
and up to 6.5 GW of BESS, reinforcing its leadership in India’s clean-energy transition. 
 
About Reliance Power 
 
www.reliancepower.co.in 
 
Reliance Power Limited, part of the Reliance Group, is one of India's leading private sector power 
generation companies. The Company has an operating portfolio of 5,305 megawatts, that 
includes 3,960 megawatts Sasan Power Limited (world's largest integrated coal based power 
plant). For the past seven years, Sasan Power has consistently ranked as the best operating 
power plant in India.
